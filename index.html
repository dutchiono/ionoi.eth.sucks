Musings

Random thoughts, lessons learned, and the occasional rant about building things.

The Gap Between Knowing and Doing

February 2026

Everyone talks about imposter syndrome like it's a bug you need to fix. I'm starting to think it's a feature. The day you stop doubting whether you're good enough is probably the day you stop getting better.

I've been thinking a lot about the gap between knowing what to do and actually doing it. You can read every tutorial, watch every video, copy every best practice ‚Äî but until you've broken something at 3 AM and had to fix it before anyone notices, you haven't really learned it.

Blue-collar work taught me that. You don't get credit for knowing the right tool exists. You get credit for grabbing it and fixing the problem.

Why I'm Building in Public

January 2026

Building in public is uncomfortable. Every half-working project, every abandoned experiment, every time I pivot ‚Äî it's all out there. But hiding until you're "ready" is a trap.

If I waited until my projects were perfect before showing them, I'd never ship anything. And honestly? The messy, in-progress stuff is more valuable than the polished final product. It shows the process, the failures, the pivots. That's the real learning.

So yeah, this site is public. The projects are public. The mistakes are public. If that's not good enough for someone, they weren't going to hire me anyway.

What I'm Actually Looking For

December 2025

People keep asking what kind of job I want. Honest answer? I have no idea. I just know I want to work with people who build things that matter and who give a shit about the details.

I don't care if it's crypto, AI, developer tools, or something I've never heard of. I care about whether the problem is interesting and whether the people solving it are the kind who stay up late fixing edge cases instead of calling it "good enough."

I'm not picky about job titles. I'm picky about who I work with.
